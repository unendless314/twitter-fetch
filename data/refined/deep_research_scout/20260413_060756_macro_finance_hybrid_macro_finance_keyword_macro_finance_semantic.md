Title: Core inflation metrics used by central banks are fundamentally broken in an era of recurring supply-side shocks.
URL: https://x.com/chigrl/status/2043088116861321540
Hidden Assumption: Food and energy price volatility is "transitory noise," and "Core" inflation (ex-food and energy) is the true signal that monetary policy should target to gauge the health of the economy.
Systemic Gap: By systematically ignoring the input costs that most affect households and production (food, energy), central banks are making policy based on a model of the economy that diverges from reality. This leads to a loss of credibility and major policy errors, as they are perpetually fighting the last war (demand-driven inflation) in a new era of supply constraints.
Required Primitive: A new inflation measurement framework robust to supply shocks. This "Real World Inflation" index would need to dynamically weigh inputs, differentiate between supply-driven and demand-driven price changes, and better reflect the non-discretionary costs faced by households and businesses.
Recommended Research Leads: Re-evaluate the justifications for excluding food/energy from the 1970s; study the second-order effects of energy price shocks on all other goods and services; develop alternative inflation models using high-frequency data (e.g., from shipping, satellite imagery) to detect supply bottlenecks in real-time.
Stance: support
Reason: This challenges the primary target variable of the most powerful financial institutions on Earth. If the Fed's ruler is wrong, every decision it makes is flawed. Designing a better inflation gauge for a world of geopolitical and climate-driven supply shocks is a foundational problem that will become increasingly urgent over the next decade.
