Title: Abundance from AI Will Not Cause Collapse Without Institutional Failure
URL: https://x.com/apralky/status/2025713692730655052
Hidden Assumption: A massive technological productivity shock (e.g., from AI) that leads to mass unemployment will inevitably cause an aggregate-demand shortfall and economic collapse.
Systemic Gap: Current macroeconomic doomsday scenarios around AI fail to distinguish between technological inevitability and policy/institutional failure. They assume that the mechanisms for translating productive capacity into purchasing power are static and will simply break, rather than adapt. The real problem is not the abundance of production ("2 loaves of bread"), but the institutional friction preventing its distribution.
Required Primitive: A "post-scarcity" or "abundance economics" policy framework that decouples purchasing power from traditional employment. This would involve proactive design of new fiscal mechanisms (e.g., large-scale transfers, wage subsidies, new tax structures on capital/AI) that can be deployed as mass unemployment begins, ensuring that aggregate demand keeps pace with AI-driven potential output.
Recommended Research Leads: Model the fiscal capacity of a government in a deflationary, high-productivity economy; design and simulate new transfer/subsidy mechanisms; study historical examples of massive labor displacement and the policy responses that succeeded or failed.
Stance: debunk
Reason: The post debunks a popular and growingly influential "techno-determinist" view of AI-induced collapse. It correctly reframes the problem as one of institutional design, which is a solvable problem, rather than an inevitable fate. It highlights that the "first principles" intuition (more stuff shouldn't make us poorer) is correct and that the failure lies in our current systems, not in the technology itself. This perspective is crucial for steering the conversation toward productive solutions.
